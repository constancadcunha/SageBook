Key Insight
The model reveals three distinct entry points based on emotional state: urgency (quick meals), curiosity (skill-building), and efficiency (meal prep). Time-pressed users often abandon apps when they can't immediately find "no-brainer" options.
üí° Design Opportunity: Create a "Rush Mode" that hides advanced features during high-stress cooking.
New Findings
Interviews with parents and busy professionals exposed time poverty as the dominant barrier:
  • 45% prioritize speed over nutrition
  • Cleanup avoidance leads to single-pot recipes
  • Ingredient waste anxiety higher among budget-conscious users

Patterns Across Personas

Tone Matters
Annie needs reassurance, Carlos craves expertise, and Tom wants directness. The app adapts its voice based on user behavior patterns.
üîÑ

Flexibility

Adaptable Guidance
All personas benefit from adjustable recipe complexity, from Annie's step-by-step to Carlos' experimental mode.

Key Takeaways

üéØ

Emotional First

Cooking is deeply tied to self-worth. Design must address emotional barriers before teaching skills.

üß©

üå±
